Week 4: Race!

The goals for week 4 were to confirm success for our past tasks (visual servoing and exploring space), and to create a system to guide our car through a race course using the skills taught and practiced in past lectures and labs. Because the task was naturally split as it was in week 3, our team split into the same subteams for efficiency. Monday and Tuesday were spent refining and optimizing programs from week 2 and 3 to prepare for the pre-race Tech Challenges. This process included everything from calibrating individual values to reworking entire algorithms.

A modified potential fields code was implemented that was better suited to navigating through clear paths than obstacle free space. The size of the forwards component was increased to increase speed, and calibrate the variables to make the ACECA follow more towards the middle of the track . A more robust algorithm for blob detection was devised and implemented. The newer program masks the image for all desired colors at the same time, picks the largest identified blob, and finds the color of that area of the image. This algorithm is more reliable than the previous one because it prioritizes size and only size, whereas the former version prioritized color arbitrarily. This was an unintentional side effect of the older program going through one color at a time, making it inefficient for only the biggest one to be taken into account.

Wednesday was spent preparing a system for the final race within Walker Memorial, which would be the real test setting. Because of that, color ranges and values for blob detection were re-calibrated to the new and final setting with success in both detection and a lack of noise. The values within the potential field controller code were re-calibrated for the new and final track. An intermediary node between all the drive controllers was created to decide which node has the most authority based on the conditions at any given time, to avoid conflict between commands or messages inappropriate for the given situation being acted on.

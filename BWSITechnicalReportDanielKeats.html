Overview:

        The four week long BeaverWorks Summer Institute (BWSI) is a summer program offered to rising high school seniors comprised of  two Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) classes. The first is the spring 2016 version of 6.141 "Robotics Science and Systems." The second is the short IAP RACECAR course. (Winter) MIT has designed to courses to teach students about robotic control systems. In total 46 students attended the program where 22 students roomed at Boston university and 24 students comminuted from neighboring Boston communities.

-  Your  technical goals  for the week

Learn to robot system and by the end of the week have the car follow a wall on the left or right side.Learn how to use a PID controller. Explain PID controller with graph of sin wave compounding on itself.

                            -  Your approach:  how did you decide to set about your work, and why?

Worked around a single person and their laptop, allowed for all member to see what was going on

                -  Your process:  step by step, what did you do?

1.read data from the Lidar, a spinning laser that uses the speed of light to find the distance from opbects. Makes a full sweep in 25ms.

2.decided what to do with the data, ended up sectioning off the points and finding the shortest distance, accounts for the car being in different angles that would change the true distance from the wall.

3. Determine how the car should react to our specific point. Move closer or farther from the wall

4. Integrated PID control that allowed the car to dynamically steer to its desired distance from the wall.

Tested the car on both left and right side. Per formed equally. The system determined how much the wheels needed to move in order to place itself in the optimal location on the track. The team learned how to interact with the car in the real world. Diving along a wall.

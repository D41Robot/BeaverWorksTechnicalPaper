BeaverWorks Summer Institute 2016 Technical Report

By: Chris Colley, Daniel Keats, Emma Vukelj

The four week long BeaverWorks Summer Institute (BWSI) is a summer program offered to rising high school seniors comprised of two Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) classes. The first is the spring 2016 version of 6.141 "Robotics Science and Systems." The second is the short IAP RACECAR course. (Winter) MIT has designed to courses to teach students about robotic control systems. In total 46 students attended the program, 22 students roomed at Boston university and 24 students comminuted from neighboring Boston communities. Together all students students worked together in Building 33 on the MIT campus. The day composed of lab time with the cars and lectures providing knowledge on how to effectively use the car.
